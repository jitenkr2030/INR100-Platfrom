## Cybersecurity Fundamentals

### Understanding Cyber Threats

#### **Common Financial Cyber Threats**
**Banking Trojans**:
- **Purpose**: Steal banking credentials and financial information
- **Delivery**: Often through email attachments or malicious websites
- **Impact**: Unauthorized access to bank accounts and transactions
- **Prevention**: Keep antivirus updated, avoid suspicious downloads

**Credential Harvesting**:
- **Method**: Fake login pages to steal usernames and passwords
- **Targets**: Online banking, investment platforms, trading accounts
- **Recognition**: Check URL carefully, look for HTTPS certificates
- **Protection**: Use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication

#### **Social Engineering Attacks**
**Pretexting**:
- **Method**: Creating false scenarios to gain trust
- **Examples**: "Your account has been compromised, verify your details"
- **Response**: Banks never ask for sensitive information via phone/email
- **Prevention**: Verify through official channels only

**Baiting**:
- **Method**: Offering something attractive to trick users
- **Examples**: "Free stock tips", "Exclusive investment opportunity"
- **Red Flags**: Promises of guaranteed returns, pressure to act quickly
- **Protection**: If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is

## Phishing and Social Engineering

### Recognizing Phishing Attacks

#### **Email Phishing Indicators**
**Sender Verification**:
- **Email Address**: Check if email address matches official domains
- **Domain Lookalikes**: Beware of slight spelling variations (e.g., gma1l.com)
- **Company Logo**: Verify if logo and branding are correct
- **Contact Information**: Cross-check contact details with official sources

**Content Red Flags**:
- **Urgent Language**: "Immediate action required", "Account suspended"
- **Threats**: Threats of account closure or legal action
- **Generic Greetings**: "Dear Customer" instead of your actual name
- **Poor Grammar**: Spelling and grammar errors in official communications

#### **Website Phishing Recognition**
**URL Verification**:
- **HTTPS**: Ensure website uses HTTPS encryption
- **Domain Accuracy**: Check if domain exactly matches official website
- **Certificate Validity**: Verify SSL certificate is valid and current
- **URL Shorteners**: Be cautious with shortened URLs

**Website Security Features**:
- **Security Badges**: Look for security certification badges
- **Privacy Policy**: Verify presence of clear privacy policy
- **Contact Information**: Check for legitimate contact information
- **Website Age**: Be suspicious of newly created domains

### Social Engineering Tactics

#### **Phone-Based Attacks**
**Vishing (Voice Phishing)**:
- **Caller ID Spoofing**: Phone numbers can be spoofed
- **Pressure Tactics**: Creating urgency to bypass critical thinking
- **Information Requests**: Asking for sensitive information over phone
- **Verification**: Hang up and call back using official numbers

**Tech Support Scams**:
- **False Alerts**: Pop-up warnings about computer issues
- **Remote Access**: Requesting remote access to your computer
- **Payment Demands**: Demanding payment for fake services
- **Protection**: Never give remote access to unknown parties

#### **Social Media Engineering**
**Friend Requests**:
- **Fake Profiles**: Profiles with limited friends and recent activity
- **Investment Claims**: Claims of guaranteed investment returns
- **Personal Information**: Asking for personal or financial details
- **Verification**: Verify identity through mutual connections

**Investment Groups**:
- **Pump Groups**: Groups promoting specific stocks
- **Pyramid Schemes**: Groups recruiting for investment opportunities
- **Insider Tips**: Claims of insider information
- **Red Flags**: Pressure to invest quickly without proper research

## Mobile Security Best Practices

### Mobile Device Security

#### **Device Protection**
**Screen Lock**:
- **Strong Passwords**: Use complex passwords, not simple patterns
- **Biometric Security**: Enable fingerprint or face recognition
- **Auto-Lock**: Set short auto-lock times
- **Remote Wipe**: Enable remote device wipe capabilities

**App Security**:
- **Official Sources**: Only download apps from official app stores
- **Permissions**: Review app permissions before installation
- **Updates**: Keep apps updated for security patches
- **Unnecessary Apps**: Remove apps you don't use

#### **Financial App Security**
**Banking Apps**:
- **Official Apps**: Only use official banking apps
- **App Permissions**: Limit permissions to essential functions
- **Session Management**: Log out after using banking apps
- **Network Security**: Avoid banking on public Wi-Fi

**Investment Apps**:
- **Platform Verification**: Verify investment platform legitimacy
- **Login Security**: Use strong passwords and 2FA
- **Transaction Alerts**: Enable transaction notifications
- **Regular Review**: Regularly review account activity

### Network Security

#### **Wi-Fi Security**
**Public Wi-Fi Risks**:
- **Man-in-the-Middle**: Attacks on public networks
- **Unencrypted Traffic**: Sensitive data transmitted in plain text
- **Fake Hotspots**: Malicious Wi-Fi networks
- **Data Interception**: Intercepting sensitive communications

**Safe Wi-Fi Practices**:
- **VPN Usage**: Use VPN on public Wi-Fi networks
- **Network Verification**: Verify legitimate Wi-Fi network names
- **Auto-Connect**: Disable auto-connect to Wi-Fi networks
- **Mobile Data**: Use mobile data for sensitive transactions

#### **Home Network Security**
**Router Security**:
- **Default Passwords**: Change default router passwords
- **Firmware Updates**: Keep router firmware updated
- **Network Encryption**: Use WPA3 encryption
- **Guest Networks**: Use separate networks for guests

**IoT Device Security**:
- **Device Updates**: Keep IoT devices updated
- **Default Passwords**: Change all default passwords
- **Network Isolation**: Isolate IoT devices from main network
- **Manufacturer Security**: Choose devices from reputable manufacturers
