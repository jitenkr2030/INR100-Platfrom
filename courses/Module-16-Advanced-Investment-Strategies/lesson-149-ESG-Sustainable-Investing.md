## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to ESG and Sustainable Investing

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) investing integrates non-financial factors into investment analysis and decision-making. Sustainable investing aims to generate positive financial returns while creating positive environmental and social impact. This approach recognizes that sustainability factors can materially affect investment performance and long-term value creation.

**Key Principle**: "ESG factors are not just about doing good - they're about identifying material risks and opportunities that impact financial performance"

## Key Takeaways

1. **ESG factors are material to financial performance** and should be integrated into investment analysis
2. **Climate risk is both a financial risk and an investment opportunity** requiring proactive management
3. **ESG data and ratings have limitations** and should be used as one input in comprehensive analysis
4. **Regulatory requirements are driving ESG standardization** but methodology differences persist
5. **Long-term ESG integration can enhance risk-adjusted returns** through better risk management and opportunity identification
