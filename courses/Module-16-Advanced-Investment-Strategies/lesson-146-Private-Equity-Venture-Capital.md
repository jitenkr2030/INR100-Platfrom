## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to Private Equity and Venture Capital

Private equity and venture capital represent alternative investment strategies that provide capital to private companies in exchange for ownership stakes. These investments offer potential for superior returns through active ownership, operational improvements, and strategic repositioning.

**Key Principle**: "Private equity and venture capital provide growth capital and expertise to create value through operational improvements, strategic positioning, and optimal exit timing"

### 2. Private Equity and Venture Capital Structures

#### 2.1 Fund Structures
**Limited Partnership (LP) Structure:**
- **General Partners (GPs)**: Fund managers and investment professionals
- **Limited Partners (LPs)**: Institutional and high-net-worth investors
- **Management Fees**: 1.5-2.5% of committed capital annually
- **Carried Interest**: 20% of profits above hurdle rate

**Fund Terms:**
- **Commitment Period**: 5-7 years for capital calls
- **Investment Period**: 3-5 years for new investments
- **Fund Life**: 10-12 years including extensions
- **Extension Options**: Limited extensions for wind-down

#### 2.2 Investment Categories
**Buyout Funds:**
- **Control Transactions**: Majority ownership positions
- **Leverage**: Significant debt financing
- **Operational Improvement**: Management and strategy
- **Exit Strategies**: Strategic sales, IPOs, secondary sales

**Growth Capital:**
- **Minority Stakes**: Non-controlling investments
- **Expansion Funding**: Growth and scale-up capital
- **Working Capital**: Operational funding
- **Market Expansion**: Geographic and product expansion

**Venture Capital:**
- **Early Stage**: Seed and Series A funding
- **Growth Stage**: Series B and beyond
- **Sector Focus**: Technology, healthcare, fintech
- **Strategic Value**: Technology and market access

### 4. Valuation Methodologies

#### 4.1 Pre-Money Valuation
**Valuation Approaches:**
- **Comparable Company Analysis**: Public and private multiples

- **Net Asset Value**: Asset-based approach

**Adjustments and Premiums:**
- **Control Premium**: Majority stake premiums
- **Illiquidity Discount**: Private company discounts
- **Size Premium**: Small company adjustments
- **Market Conditions**: Economic environment factors

#### 4.2 Post-Money and Investment Structure
**Capital Structure:**
- **Equity Investment**: Primary equity stakes

### 10. Fund Performance and Analysis

#### 10.1 Performance Metrics
**IRR Calculation:**
- **Time-Weighted Returns**: Cash flow timing impact
- **Net IRR**: After fees and expenses
- **Gross IRR**: Before fees and expenses
- **J-Curve**: Early negative cash flows

**Multiple Analysis:**
- **MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital)**: Total return multiple
- **TVPI (Total Value to Paid-In)**: Total value including residual
- **DPI (Distributions to Paid-In)**: Cash returned to investors
- **RVPI (Residual Value to Paid-In)**: Remaining portfolio value
