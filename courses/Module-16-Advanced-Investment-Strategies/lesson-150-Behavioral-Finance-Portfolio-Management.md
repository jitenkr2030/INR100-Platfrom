## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to Behavioral Finance

Behavioral finance examines the psychological factors that influence financial decisions, challenging the traditional assumption of rational investors. Understanding behavioral biases is crucial for portfolio management as it helps identify opportunities to improve decision-making and create more robust investment strategies.

**Key Principle**: "Markets are not always efficient because investors are not always rational. Behavioral insights create opportunities for better portfolio management and investment outcomes"

### 2. Cognitive Biases in Investment Decisions

#### 2.1 Cognitive Biases
**Confirmation Bias:**
- **Definition**: Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs
- **Impact**: Ignores contrary evidence and risk signals
- **Portfolio Impact**: Overconfidence in holdings, delayed exit
- **Mitigation**: Devil's advocate analysis, contrarian research

**Availability Heuristic:**
- **Definition**: Overweighting recent or memorable information
- **Impact**: Overreaction to recent events and news
- **Portfolio Impact**: Recency bias, panic selling, momentum chasing
- **Mitigation**: Historical perspective, statistical analysis

**Representativeness Heuristic:**
- **Definition**: Categorizing based on stereotypes
- **Impact**: Grouping stocks incorrectly, missing opportunities
- **Portfolio Impact**: Sector bias, style bias, classification errors
- **Mitigation**: Data-driven analysis, multiple perspectives

#### 2.2 Emotional Biases
**Loss Aversion:**
- **Definition**: Losses hurt more than equivalent gains feel good
- **Impact**: Holding losers too long, selling winners too early
- **Portfolio Impact**: Portfolio drift, tax inefficiency
- **Mitigation**: Systematic rebalancing, stop-loss discipline

**Mental Accounting:**
- **Definition**: Treating money differently based on source/purpose
- **Impact**: Suboptimal allocation, irrational decisions
- **Portfolio Impact**: Poor asset allocation, forced savings
- **Mitigation**: Holistic portfolio view, total return focus

**Anchoring:**
- **Definition**: Over-relying on first piece of information
- **Impact**: Sticking to purchase prices, ignoring current value
- **Portfolio Impact**: Unrealized gains, loss realization issues
- **Mitigation**: Current market valuation, opportunity cost analysis
