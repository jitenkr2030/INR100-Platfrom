## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to International Investing

International investing involves allocating capital to assets and securities outside one's home country. It provides diversification benefits, access to growth opportunities, and exposure to different economic cycles. However, it also introduces currency risk, geopolitical uncertainties, and regulatory complexities.

**Key Principle**: "International diversification can reduce portfolio risk and enhance returns, but requires careful currency and risk management"

### 2. Benefits of International Diversification

#### 2.1 Risk Reduction Through Diversification
**Correlation Benefits:**
- **Low Cross-Country Correlations**: Different economic cycles and market dynamics
- **Asynchronous Business Cycles**: Economic growth timing differences
- **Industry Specialization**: Access to unique sector exposures
- **Currency Diversification**: Multiple currency exposures

**Empirical Evidence:**
- **Historical Performance**: International portfolios show lower volatility
- **Efficient Frontier**: Better risk-return combinations
- **Crisis Benefits**: Home country bias elimination during local crises
- **Long-term Enhancement**: Sustained diversification benefits

### 4. Currency Risk Management

#### 4.1 Types of Currency Risk
**Transaction Risk:**
- **Import/Export Exposure**: Foreign currency payments/receipts
- **Investment Cash Flows**: Foreign investment income
- **Operational Exposure**: Subsidiary operations
- **Time Horizon**: Exposure duration and timing

**Translation Risk:**
- **Consolidation**: Foreign subsidiary financial statements
- **Reporting Requirements**: Parent company reporting
- **Asset/Liability Matching**: Currency denomination differences
- **Earnings Volatility**: Currency impact on reported results

**Economic Risk:**
- **Competitive Position**: Currency impact on competitiveness
- **Market Share**: Price competitiveness in global markets
- **Cash Flow Impact**: Long-term business value effects
- **Strategic Planning**: Investment and operational decisions

#### 4.2 Currency Risk Measurement
**Value at Risk (VaR) for Currency:**
- **Historical VaR**: Using past currency return distributions
- **Parametric VaR**: Normal distribution assumptions
- **Monte Carlo VaR**: Simulation-based risk measurement
- **Stress Testing**: Extreme scenario analysis

**Correlation Analysis:**
- **Currency Correlations**: Cross-currency relationship analysis
- **Asset-Currency Correlation**: Investment and currency relationship
- **Dynamic Correlations**: Time-varying correlation patterns
- **Crisis Correlations**: Correlation changes during stress periods

### 5. Currency Hedging Strategies

#### 5.1 Forward Contracts
**Basic Forward Hedging:**
- **Lock-in Rates**: Eliminate currency uncertainty
- **Cost Structure**: Forward premium/discount analysis
- **Hedge Ratio**: Percentage of exposure to hedge
- **Timing**: Optimal hedge initiation timing

**Rolling Hedge Strategy:**
- **Continuous Hedging**: Regular hedge renewals
- **Hedge Effectiveness**: Perfect vs imperfect hedges
- **Cost Management**: Long-term hedging cost optimization
- **Performance Attribution**: Currency vs investment returns

#### 5.2 Options-Based Hedging
**Protective Put Strategy:**
- **Downside Protection**: Limit currency losses
- **Upside Participation**: Maintain currency gains potential
- **Premium Cost**: Option premium payment
- **Strike Selection**: At-the-money vs out-of-the-money

**Collar Strategy:**
- **Cost Reduction**: Fund put with call sale
- **Participation Limits**: Capped upside and downside
- **Hedge Effectiveness**: Partial protection with cost savings
- **Strike Optimization**: Balance protection and cost

#### 5.3 Currency Swaps
**Long-term Hedging:**
- **Principal Exchange**: Exchange principal amounts
- **Interest Rate Swaps**: Long-term interest rate hedging
- **Commercial Swaps**: Bank-facilitated hedging
- **Regulatory Treatment**: Accounting and tax implications
