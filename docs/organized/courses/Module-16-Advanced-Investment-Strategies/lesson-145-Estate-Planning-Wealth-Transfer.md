## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to Estate Planning

Estate planning is the process of arranging for the management and disposal of an individual's assets during their lifetime and after death. It ensures wealth preservation, minimizes taxes, provides for family needs, and maintains control over asset distribution according to personal wishes.

**Key Principle**: "Estate planning is not just about death - it's about protecting and preserving wealth throughout life while ensuring smooth transfer to the next generation"

### 2. Estate Planning Fundamentals

#### 2.1 Estate Planning Objectives
**Wealth Preservation:**
- **Asset Protection**: Safeguarding wealth from claims
- **Tax Minimization**: Reducing estate and inheritance taxes
- **Inflation Protection**: Maintaining real value
- **Family Security**: Ensuring family financial security

**Control and Distribution:**
- **Personal Wishes**: Following individual preferences
- **Family Harmony**: Avoiding disputes and conflicts
- **Charitable Goals**: Supporting charitable causes
- **Legacy Building**: Creating lasting impact

#### 2.2 Legal Framework in India
**Hindu Succession Act:**
- **Applicability**: Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, Sikh families
- **Class I Heirs**: Children, spouse, parents
- **Class II Heirs**: Brothers, sisters, grandparents
- **Class III Heirs**: Cousins and other relatives

**Indian Succession Act:**
- **Applicability**: Christians, Parsis, Muslims
- **Testamentary Succession**: Will-based inheritance
- **Intestate Succession**: Without will inheritance
- **Probate Process**: Court validation requirements
