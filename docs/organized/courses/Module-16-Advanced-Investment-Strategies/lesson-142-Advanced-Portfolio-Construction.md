## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to Advanced Portfolio Construction

Portfolio construction is the systematic process of combining different assets to achieve specific investment objectives while managing risk. Advanced portfolio construction goes beyond simple diversification to include optimization techniques, risk budgeting, and sophisticated rebalancing strategies.

**Key Principle**: "Portfolio construction is more important than individual security selection in determining long-term returns"

### 2. Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) Foundation

#### 2.1 Efficient Frontier Concept
**Mathematical Foundation:**
- **Expected Return**: Weighted average of asset returns
- **Portfolio Variance**: Combination of individual variances and correlations
- **Optimization**: Maximize return for given risk level
- **Efficient Set**: All optimal portfolios

**Practical Application:**
- Identify optimal portfolios along efficient frontier
- Select portfolio based on risk tolerance
- Understand trade-offs between risk and return
- Regular rebalancing to maintain efficiency

#### 2.2 Correlation and Diversification
**Correlation Matrix Analysis:**
- **Correlation Coefficient**: Ranges from -1 to +1
- **Diversification Benefit**: Lower portfolio volatility
- **Optimal Correlation**: Near zero correlation ideal
- **Dynamic Relationships**: Correlation changes over time

**Asset Class Correlations in Indian Markets:**
- **Equity vs Bonds**: 0.1 to 0.3 (low correlation)
- **Gold vs Equity**: -0.2 to 0.2 (hedge potential)
- **Real Estate vs Equity**: 0.4 to 0.6 (moderate correlation)
- **International vs Domestic**: 0.3 to 0.5 (geographic diversification)

**Long-term Policy Allocation:**
- **Target Weights**: Based on investment objectives

**Indian Market Asset Classes:**

#### 3.2 Tactical Asset Allocation
**Market-Timing Approach:**
- **Economic Indicators**: Leading economic indicators
- **Valuation Metrics**: P/E ratios, yield spreads
- **Market Sentiment**: Technical indicators, surveys
- **Risk-on/Risk-off**: Sentiment-based allocation

**Implementation Framework:**
- **Allocation Bands**: Allowable deviation from strategic weights
- **Decision Process**: Systematic approach to allocation changes
- **Risk Management**: Maximum exposure limits
- **Performance Attribution**: Source of excess returns

### 4. Risk Management in Portfolio Construction

#### 4.1 Risk Budgeting
**Risk Factor Exposure:**
- **Market Risk**: Systematic risk exposure
- **Size Risk**: Small-cap vs large-cap exposure
- **Value vs Growth**: Style factor exposure
- **Sector Risk**: Industry concentration limits

**Risk Metrics:**
- **Value at Risk (VaR)**: Maximum expected loss
- **Conditional VaR**: Average loss beyond VaR
- **Tracking Error**: Deviation from benchmark
- **Beta**: Systematic risk measure

#### 4.2 Position Sizing and Concentration
**Kelly Criterion Application:**
```
f = (bp - q) / b
Where: f = fraction of capital to bet
       b = odds received on the wager
       p = probability of winning
       q = probability of losing (1-p)
```

**Practical Position Sizing:**
- **Risk Parity**: Equal risk contribution from each position
- **Volatility-Based**: Position size inversely related to volatility
- **Confidence-Based**: Size based on conviction level
- **Diversification-Based**: Maximum position limits

### 7. Rebalancing Strategies

#### 7.1 Rebalancing Methods
**Time-Based Rebalancing:**
- **Calendar Rebalancing**: Quarterly, semi-annual, annual
- **Threshold Rebalancing**: When allocations deviate by X%
- **Combination Approach**: Time and threshold triggers

**Tax-Aware Rebalancing:**
- **Tax-Loss Harvesting**: Realize losses to offset gains
- **Asset Location**: Tax-efficient account placement
- **Wash Sale Rules**: Avoid 30-day rule violations
- **Dividend Timing**: Harvest dividends strategically

#### 7.2 Rebalancing Implementation
**Cost-Benefit Analysis:**
- **Transaction Costs**: Brokerage and market impact
- **Tax Consequences**: Capital gains and losses
- **Market Timing**: Opportunity cost of waiting
- **Implementation Shortfall**: Best execution vs market impact

### 8. Performance Attribution and Analysis

#### 8.1 Attribution Framework
**Brinson Model:**
- **Allocation Effect**: Asset allocation decisions
- **Selection Effect**: Security selection decisions
- **Interaction Effect**: Combined allocation and selection
- **Residual Effect**: Unexplained performance

**Factor Attribution:**
- **Market Factor**: Beta exposure
- **Style Factors**: Value, growth, size, momentum
- **Industry Factors**: Sector allocation
- **Security Selection**: Idiosyncratic returns

#### 8.2 Risk-Adjusted Performance
**Sharpe Ratio:**
```
Sharpe Ratio = (Portfolio Return - Risk-free Rate) / Portfolio Volatility
```

**Information Ratio:**
```
Information Ratio = Excess Return / Tracking Error
```

**Maximum Drawdown:**
- **Peak-to-Trough Decline**: Worst loss period
- **Recovery Time**: Time to regain peak value
- **Ulcer Index**: Depth and duration of drawdowns

### 9. Advanced Optimization Techniques

#### 9.1 Black-Litterman Model
**Combining Market Equilibrium with Views:**
- **Market Capitalization Weights**: Implied equilibrium returns
- **Investor Views**: Override market expectations
- **Confidence Levels**: Weight given to views
- **Optimal Portfolio**: Market equilibrium + views

#### 9.2 Risk Parity Approach
**Equal Risk Contribution:**
- **Risk Contribution**: Each asset contributes equally to portfolio risk
- **Volatility Targeting**: Adjust weights based on volatility
- **Correlation Adjustments**: Account for changing correlations
- **Dynamic Rebalancing**: Regular risk contribution rebalancing
