# Module 2: Indian Financial System

## Course Overview

This comprehensive module provides an in-depth understanding of India's financial system architecture, institutions, and mechanisms. Building on the foundation of Money Basics, this course explores how the Indian financial ecosystem operates, from banking institutions to capital markets, regulatory frameworks, and emerging payment systems.

## Course Structure

### Target Audience
- Students who completed Module 1 (Money Basics)
- Banking and finance professionals seeking system understanding
- Business professionals working with financial institutions
- Investors wanting to understand market infrastructure
- Anyone interested in India's economic and financial landscape

### Learning Objectives
Upon completion of this module, students will:
- Understand the structure and functions of Indian financial institutions
- Comprehend RBI's role in monetary policy and financial stability
- Learn about banking system operations and types of banks
- Explore capital markets, stock exchanges, and securities regulation
- Understand payment systems, digital transactions, and financial inclusion
- Analyze the regulatory framework governing Indian finance

## Course Curriculum

### Lesson 01: Overview of Indian Financial System
**Focus**: System architecture and institutional framework
- Structure of Indian financial system
- Financial institutions and their roles
- Financial markets and instruments
- Regulatory bodies and governance
- Evolution and current state

### Lesson 02: Reserve Bank of India - Central Banking Functions
**Focus**: RBI's role as India's central bank
- RBI's establishment and evolution
- Core functions and responsibilities
- Monetary policy tools and implementation
- Currency management and note printing
- Financial stability and supervision

### Lesson 03: Banking System Structure and Types
**Focus**: Commercial banking and cooperative banks
- Public sector banks structure and functions
- Private sector banks and their operations
- Foreign banks in India
- Cooperative banks and regional rural banks
- Payment banks and small finance banks

### Lesson 04: Development Finance Institutions
**Focus**: Specialized lending and development banking
- Development Finance Institution (DFI) concept
- Major DFIs: SIDBI, NABARD, NHB, Exim Bank
- Infrastructure financing and project finance
- Microfinance and financial inclusion
- Government-backed financing schemes

### Lesson 05: Capital Markets and Stock Exchanges
**Focus**: Equity and debt market infrastructure
- Stock exchanges: NSE, BSE, and regional exchanges
- Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) role
- Market intermediaries: brokers, advisors, research
- Trading mechanisms and settlement systems
- Market regulation and investor protection

### Lesson 06: Debt Markets and Government Securities
**Focus**: Government and corporate debt markets
- Government securities market structure
- Treasury bills, government bonds, and state loans
- Corporate bond market and rating agencies
- Mutual funds and debt schemes
- Bond trading and yield curve

### Lesson 07: Insurance and Pension Sector
**Focus**: Insurance companies and pension funds
- Life and general insurance market structure
- Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority (IRDAI)
- Pension fund management and NPS
- Annuity and retirement products
- Micro-insurance and social security

### Lesson 08: Payment Systems and Digital Finance
**Focus**: Modern payment infrastructure
- Payment systems: RTGS, NEFT, IMPS, UPI
- Digital payments and fintech ecosystem
- Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)
- Financial inclusion through technology
- Payment aggregators and banks

### Lesson 09: Regulatory Framework and Supervision
**Focus**: Financial regulation and compliance
- Financial sector regulators: RBI, SEBI, IRDAI, PFRDA
- Banking regulation and prudential norms
- Securities market regulation and compliance
- Insurance regulation and solvency requirements
- Anti-money laundering and KYC requirements

### Lesson 10: Financial Inclusion and Policy Initiatives
**Focus**: Expanding financial access and services
- Financial inclusion definitions and strategies
- Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana and similar schemes
- Digital India and cashless economy initiatives
- Banking correspondent and business correspondent models
- Future trends and challenges in Indian finance
