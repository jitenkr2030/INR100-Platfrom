## What is Stock Market Fraud?

Stock market fraud involves deceptive practices that manipulate stock prices or mislead investors for financial gain. Fraudsters often target retail investors who lack deep market knowledge.

### Common Types of Stock Market Fraud

#### 1. **Insider Trading**
- **What it is**: Trading based on non-public, material information
- **Why it's illegal**: Gives unfair advantage and violates market integrity
- **Red flags**: Sudden large trades before major announcements

#### 2. **Market Manipulation**
- **What it is**: Artificial inflation or deflation of stock prices
- **Methods**: Wash trading, spoofing, layering
- **Victims**: Retail investors who buy at inflated prices

#### 3. **Investment Advisory Fraud**
- **What it is**: False or misleading investment advice
- **Examples**: Guaranteed returns, insider tips, "secret" strategies
- **Warning**: No one can guarantee investment returns

## How Fraudsters Target Retail Investors

### 1. **Social Media Scams**
- **Platforms**: WhatsApp, Telegram, Facebook groups
- **Common tactic**: "Insider tips" from "successful traders"
- **Reality**: These are usually pump-and-dump schemes

### 2. **Cold Calling**
- **Approach**: Random phone calls with investment opportunities
- **Common claims**: "We have connections" or "Secret information"
- **Reality**: Legitimate advisors don't cold call random people

### 3. **Email Scams**
- **Method**: Unsolicited emails with "hot stock tips"
- **Red flags**: Poor grammar, urgent language, unrealistic claims
- **Safe approach**: Delete suspicious emails immediately

### 4. **Fake News**
- **Method**: Creating false news stories to manipulate prices
- **Warning signs**: Unknown sources, dramatic headlines
- **Safe approach**: Verify news from multiple reliable sources

## Case Study: The "Insider Trading" Scam

### The Scam
A person claiming to have "insider information" about a pharmaceutical company contacted investors via WhatsApp, promising 300% returns within 2 months.

### Red Flags Missed
- Guaranteed returns
- Pressure to invest quickly
- No verifiable credentials
- Request for payment via crypto

### The Outcome
- Stock prices were artificially inflated
- Early investors made small profits
- Later investors lost 90% of their money
- Scammer disappeared with the money

### Lessons Learned
1. **Insider trading is illegal** - legitimate insiders can't share information
2. **Guaranteed returns are always suspicious**
3. **Pressure tactics indicate fraud**
4. **Always verify before investing**

## Key Takeaways

### Remember These Points
1. **Stock market fraud is common** and targets retail investors

3. **Always verify** credentials and regulatory compliance
4. **Use official channels** and registered intermediaries only
5. **Trust your instincts** - if it feels wrong, it probably is
