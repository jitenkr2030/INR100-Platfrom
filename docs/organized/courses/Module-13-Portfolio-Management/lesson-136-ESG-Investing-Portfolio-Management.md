# Lesson 136: ESG Investing & Portfolio Management

## Understanding ESG Investing

ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investing considers:
- **Environmental**: Climate change, resource use, pollution
- **Social**: Labor practices, community relations, human rights
- **Governance**: Board structure, executive compensation, ethics

## ESG Investment Strategies

### Screening Strategies
#### Negative Screening
- **Exclusion Lists**: Remove companies from investment universe
- **Industry Exclusions**: Tobacco, weapons, fossil fuels
- **Norm-Based**: Exclude companies violating international norms
- **Threshold Screening**: Minimum ESG score requirements

#### Positive Screening
- **Best-in-Class**: Select top ESG performers within sectors
- **Best-in-Progress**: Select improving ESG companies
- **Impact Investing**: Focus on positive social/environmental impact
- **Thematic Investing**: Target specific ESG themes

### ESG Integration
#### Integration Methods
- **Financial Materiality**: Include ESG in financial analysis
- **Risk Assessment**: ESG risks in investment decisions
- **Opportunity Identification**: ESG-related growth opportunities
- **Portfolio Construction**: ESG factors in allocation decisions

#### Quantitative Integration
- **ESG Scoring**: Quantitative ESG rating systems
- **Factor Models**: ESG factors in return models
- **Portfolio Optimization**: Include ESG constraints
- **Risk Models**: ESG risk factors
