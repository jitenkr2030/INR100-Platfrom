## Understanding Capital Gains

### What are Capital Gains?
Capital gains are profits realized from the sale of capital assets such as:
- **Stocks and shares**: Equity investments
- **Mutual funds**: Equity and debt funds
- **Real estate**: Property transactions
- **Bonds and debentures**: Fixed income securities
- **Gold and precious metals**: Commodity investments
- **Cryptocurrency**: Digital assets (if applicable)

### Types of Capital Gains

#### 1. **Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG)**
**Definition**: Gains from assets held for less than specified period
**Tax Rate**: According to income tax slabs (5%, 20%, 30%)
**Holding Periods**:
- Equity shares/mutual funds: Less than 1 year
- Debt mutual funds: Less than 3 years
- Real estate: Less than 2 years
- Bonds: Less than 1 year

#### 2. **Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG)**
**Definition**: Gains from assets held for specified period or more
**Tax Rate**: Preferential rates with exemptions
**Holding Periods**:
- Equity shares/mutual funds: 1 year or more (10% above â‚¹1 lakh)
- Debt mutual funds: 3 years or more (20% with indexation)
- Real estate: 2 years or more (20% with indexation)
- Bonds: 1 year or more (20% with indexation)

### Indexation Benefit for Equity
**Not Available**: Equity investments don't get indexation benefit

**Comparison**: 10% LTCG vs 20-30% STCG = significant tax saving

#### How Indexation Works
**Purpose**: Protect gains from inflation erosion
**Method**: Adjust purchase price using Cost Inflation Index (CII)

## Real Estate Taxation

## Special Investment Tax Situations

### 1. **Inheritance and Gift Taxation**

#### Inherited Assets
**Cost Basis**: Fair market value at inheritance date
**Holding Period**: Starts from inheritance date
**Tax Treatment**: Same as purchased assets

#### Gift Taxation
**Gift Tax Rules**:
- Gifts above â‚¹50,000 taxable as income
- Gifts from relatives generally tax-free
- Gift of assets may have capital gains implications

### 2. **Corporate Actions and Tax**

#### Stock Splits and Bonus Shares
**Tax Treatment**:
- No immediate tax implications
- Cost basis adjusts for additional shares
- Holding period includes original shares

#### Rights Issues
**Tax Treatment**:
- Rights shares taxed at issue price
- No immediate tax on allotment
- Cost basis includes rights price

#### Mergers and Acquisitions
**Tax Treatment**:
- Generally tax-neutral if structured properly
- Cost basis transfers to new shares
- Holding period continues

### 3. **Foreign Investment Taxation**

#### Foreign Direct Investment
**Tax Treatment**:
- Same as domestic investments
- Currency fluctuation impact
- Repatriation rules apply

#### Foreign Portfolio Investment
**Tax Treatment**:
- Equity: 10% LTCG, income tax slab STCG
- Debt: Indexation benefits available
- Currency conversion impact on gains
