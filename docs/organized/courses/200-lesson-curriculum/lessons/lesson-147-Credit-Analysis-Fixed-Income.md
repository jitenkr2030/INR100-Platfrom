## Core Content

### 1. Introduction to Credit Analysis

Credit analysis is the systematic process of evaluating the creditworthiness of borrowers and the quality of credit instruments. It encompasses the assessment of default risk, recovery prospects, and credit spreads, forming the foundation for fixed income investing and credit risk management.

**Key Principle**: "Credit analysis transforms financial information into credit risk assessments that determine investment decisions and portfolio construction"

### 2. Credit Analysis Framework

#### 2.1 Fundamental Analysis
**Financial Statement Analysis:**
- **Profitability**: Earnings trends and margin analysis
- **Liquidity**: Current ratio, quick ratio, cash conversion
- **Leverage**: Debt-to-equity, debt-to-assets, interest coverage
- **Cash Flow**: Operating cash flow quality and sustainability

**Business Analysis:**
- **Industry Assessment**: Sector characteristics and outlook
- **Competitive Position**: Market share and competitive advantages
- **Management Quality**: Track record and strategic capability
- **Corporate Governance**: Board independence and transparency

**Quantitative Ratios:**
- **Coverage Ratios**: Interest coverage, debt service coverage
- **Leverage Ratios**: Total debt, net debt metrics
- **Liquidity Ratios**: Current ratio, cash conversion cycle
- **Profitability Ratios**: ROA, ROE, operating margins

#### 2.2 Qualitative Assessment
**Industry Risk Factors:**
- **Cyclical Nature**: Business cycle sensitivity
- **Regulatory Environment**: Policy impact and changes
- **Technological Disruption**: Industry transformation risk
- **Environmental Factors**: Climate and sustainability risks

**Company-Specific Risks:**
- **Business Model**: Revenue model sustainability
- **Geographic Concentration**: Market diversification
- **Customer Concentration**: Dependency on key customers
- **Supply Chain**: Vendor and supplier dependencies

### 3. Credit Risk Assessment

#### 3.1 Default Probability Models
**Structural Models (Merton Model):**
- **Asset Value**: Firm value and volatility estimation
- **Default Boundary**: Debt service capability threshold
- **Time to Default**: Probability of default over time
- **Recovery Rate**: Expected recovery given default

**Reduced-Form Models:**
- **Hazard Rate**: Instantaneous default probability
- **Credit Spreads**: Default risk premium calibration
- **Jumps**: Credit event modeling
- **Intensity Models**: Poisson process applications

**Statistical Models:**
- **Logistic Regression**: Default probability estimation
- **Machine Learning**: Advanced statistical techniques
- **Stress Testing**: Scenario-based default analysis
- **Historical Analysis**: Empirical default patterns

#### 3.2 Credit Rating Analysis
**Rating Agency Frameworks:**
- **S&P**: Global scale and methodologies
- **Moody's**: Rating factors and scales
- **Fitch**: Rating criteria and processes
- **CRISIL**: Indian credit rating framework

**Rating Factors:**
- **Business Risk**: Industry and competitive position
- **Financial Risk**: Financial profile and flexibility
- **Liquidity**: Liquidity position and access
- **Management Quality**: Strategic and operational capability

**Rating Migration Analysis:**
- **Historical Transitions**: Rating change patterns
- **Industry Patterns**: Sector-specific migration
- **Economic Cycles**: Cycle impact on ratings
- **Model Validation**: Rating model accuracy

### 5. Credit Spread Analysis

### 8. Credit Trading Strategies

#### 8.1 Relative Value Strategies
**Credit Curve Trades:**
- **Curve Steepener**: Long front-end, short back-end
- **Curve Flattener**: Long back-end, short front-end
- **Barbell Strategy**: Long front-end and back-end
- **Butterfly Trades**: Curvature strategies

**Sector Rotation:**
- **Sector Overweight**: Favor attractive sectors
- **Sector Underweight**: Avoid weak sectors
- **Sector Spreads**: Relative value between sectors
- **Cyclical vs Defensive**: Economic cycle positioning

#### 8.2 Credit Spread Strategies
**Spread Compression:**
- **Long Spread**: Expect spread narrowing
- **Credit Selection**: Pick improving credits
- **Event-Driven**: M&A and restructuring plays
- **Rating Upgrade**: Capitalize on rating changes

**Spread Expansion:**
- **Short Spread**: Expect spread widening
- **Defensive Positioning**: Flight to quality
- **Liquidity Plays**: Market liquidity premium
- **Recovery Trades**: Post-default recovery
