## What is a Mutual Fund Factsheet?

**Mutual Fund Factsheet** is a comprehensive document that provides detailed information about a mutual fund scheme. It's updated monthly and serves as the primary source of information for investors to understand fund characteristics, performance, holdings, and risk profile.

### Key Purposes:
- **Transparency**: Full disclosure of fund details
- **Performance Tracking**: Historical returns and metrics
- **Portfolio Analysis**: Current holdings and allocation
- **Risk Assessment**: Various risk indicators
- **Investment Decision**: Data for fund selection
- **Monitoring**: Track fund progress over time

#### **Understanding Returns Data**:

```
- Red (negative): Fund underperformed benchmark
```

### 3. **Risk and Volatility Metrics**

#### **Risk Indicators**:
```
Risk Metrics (3 Year):
Standard Deviation: 18.5%
Beta: 0.92
Sharpe Ratio: 0.85
R-Squared: 0.88
Alpha: 2.3%
Maximum Drawdown: -22.1%

Volatility Classification:
Low: <12%
Moderate: 12-18%
High: >18%

Risk Rating: MODERATE
```

#### **Risk Metrics Explanation**:
```
Standard Deviation:
- Measures return volatility
- 18.5% means returns can vary by ±18.5%
- Higher number = higher volatility

Beta:
- Measures sensitivity to market movements
- 0.92 means 8% less volatile than market
- <1 = Less risky than market
- >1 = More risky than market

Sharpe Ratio:
- Risk-adjusted return measure
- 0.85 is good (>0.5 is acceptable)
- Higher is better

Alpha:
- Excess return over benchmark
- 2.3% means fund added 2.3% above market
- Positive alpha = good fund management
```


#### **Statistics Interpretation**:
```
AUM (Assets Under Management):
- Total money invested in the fund
- ₹45,678 crore is very large
- Larger funds may have economies of scale
- Very small funds may face closure risk

NAV (Net Asset Value):
- Price per unit of the fund
- ₹89.75 means one unit costs ₹89.75
- Not comparable across different funds
- Historical trend more important than absolute value

Expense Ratio:
- Annual cost of investing
- 2.25% for regular, 1.35% for direct
- Lower is better for net returns
- Significant impact on long-term wealth

Portfolio Turnover:
- Percentage of portfolio changed annually
- 28% means 28% of holdings changed
- Lower turnover = lower costs
- High turnover may indicate active management
```

## Red Flags in Factsheets

### 1. **Performance Issues**

#### **Warning Signs**:
```
Negative Red Flags:
1. Consistent underperformance vs benchmark
2. High volatility without corresponding returns
3. Large drawdowns without recovery
4. Poor risk-adjusted returns (low Sharpe)
5. Inconsistent performance across cycles

Example of Concerning Data:
- 3 Year Return: 8.5% vs Benchmark: 12.1%
- Standard Deviation: 25% (High)
- Maximum Drawdown: -35%
- Sharpe Ratio: 0.25 (Poor)
```

### 2. **Portfolio Concerns**

#### **Concentration Risks**:
```
Red Flags:
1. Top 10 holdings >70%
2. Single stock >15%
3. Single sector >35%
4. No clear sector strategy
5. High portfolio turnover (>100%)

Assessment:
- Excessive concentration increases risk
- Poor diversification
- Manager may be taking excessive risks
- May not align with stated strategy
```

### 3. **Fund Health Issues**

#### **Structural Concerns**:
```
Warning Signs:
1. Declining AUM over time
2. High expense ratio for category
3. Frequent manager changes
4. Poor liquidity
5. No clear investment strategy

Example:
- AUM declined 30% in 2 years
- Expense ratio: 3.5% vs category avg: 2.2%
- 3 different managers in 2 years
- Fund may be facing closure risk
```

## Using Factsheets for Fund Selection

### 1. **Comparative Analysis**

#### **Fund Comparison Framework**:
```
Comparison Matrix:

Criteria | Fund A | Fund B | Fund C
---------|--------|--------|--------
1 Year Return | 18.7% | 16.2% | 19.1%
3 Year Return | 14.2% | 15.8% | 13.9%
Expense Ratio | 2.25% | 1.85% | 2.45%
Sharpe Ratio | 0.85 | 0.92 | 0.78
Max Drawdown | -22.1% | -18.5% | -25.2%
AUM (₹cr) | 45,678 | 23,456 | 67,890

Analysis:
- Fund B has best risk-adjusted returns
- Fund A has moderate performance
- Fund C has highest returns but highest risk
- Choose based on risk tolerance
```

### 2. **Goal-Based Selection**

#### **Different Goals, Different Metrics**:
```
For Long-term Wealth Creation:
- Focus: 5+ year returns
- Priority: Alpha generation
- Metrics: Consistent performance, low volatility
- Example: Fund with 13%+ 5-year returns

For Regular Income:
- Focus: Dividend yield and stability
- Priority: Consistent income
- Metrics: Lower volatility, dividend history
- Example: Dividend-oriented funds

For Tax Savings:
- Focus: 3-year lock-in performance
- Priority: Tax efficiency and returns
- Metrics: ELSS category performance
- Example: ELSS funds with good track record
```

### 3. **Risk Profile Matching**

#### **Risk-Based Selection**:
```
Conservative Investor:
- Lower volatility (<15% std dev)
- Higher Sharpe ratio (>0.7)
- Lower maximum drawdown (<20%)
- Stable performance

Moderate Investor:
- Moderate volatility (15-20% std dev)
- Good Sharpe ratio (0.6-0.9)
- Balanced risk-return
- Consistent performance

Aggressive Investor:
- Higher volatility acceptable (>20% std dev)
- Focus on returns over risk metrics
- Higher growth potential
- Can tolerate larger drawdowns
```
