## What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.

### Simple vs Compound Interest

#### Simple Interest
**Formula**: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time
**Calculation**: Interest is calculated only on the original amount

**Example**: ₹10,000 at 10% per year for 5 years
- Year 1: ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000
- Year 2: ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000
- Year 3: ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000
- Year 4: ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000
- Year 5: ₹10,000 × 10% = ₹1,000

**Total Interest**: ₹5,000
**Final Amount**: ₹15,000

#### Compound Interest
**Formula**: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
Where:
- A = Final amount
- P = Principal (initial amount)
- r = Annual interest rate
- n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
- t = Time in years

**Example**: ₹10,000 at 10% per year, compounded annually for 5 years
- Year 1: ₹10,000 × 1.10 = ₹11,000
- Year 2: ₹11,000 × 1.10 = ₹12,100
- Year 3: ₹12,100 × 1.10 = ₹13,310
- Year 4: ₹13,310 × 1.10 = ₹14,641
- Year 5: ₹14,641 × 1.10 = ₹16,105

**Total Interest**: ₹6,105
**Final Amount**: ₹16,105

**The Difference**: ₹1,105 more with compound interest!

## The Magic of Compounding

### The Rule of 72
A simple way to estimate how long it takes for your money to double:
**Years to Double = 72 ÷ Annual Interest Rate**

**Examples**:
- 6% interest rate: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years
- 8% interest rate: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years
- 12% interest rate: 72 ÷ 6 = 6 years

## Common Compounding Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Starting Too Late
**Problem**: Waiting for "the right time" to start investing
**Reality**: The right time was yesterday; the next best time is today

**Solution**: Start with whatever amount you can afford, even ₹1,000 per month

### Mistake 2: Stopping During Down Markets
**Problem**: Pausing investments during market corrections
**Reality**: Market downturns are the best time to buy more at lower prices

**Solution**: Continue investing regardless of market conditions

### Mistake 3: Withdrawing Early
**Problem**: Cashing out investments before they mature
**Reality**: Short-term volatility can mask long-term growth

**Solution**: Define your investment horizon clearly and stick to it

### Mistake 4: Not Reinvesting Returns
**Problem**: Spending dividend and interest income instead of reinvesting
**Reality**: Compounding requires reinvestment to work effectively

**Solution**: Choose growth options for long-term investments

## Calculating Your Own Compound Interest

### Using the Compound Interest Formula

**Formula**: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:
- A = Final amount
- P = Principal (starting amount)
- r = Annual interest rate (as decimal)
- n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
- t = Time in years

**Example Calculation**:
- Principal: ₹1,00,000
- Annual rate: 12% (0.12)
- Compounding: Annually (n=1)
- Time: 10 years

**Calculation**: A = 100000(1 + 0.12/1)^(1×10) = ₹3,10,585

### Using SIP Calculation

**Formula for SIP**: FV = P × [((1 + i)^n - 1) / i] × (1 + i)

Where:
- FV = Future Value
- P = Monthly SIP amount
- i = Monthly interest rate (annual rate/12)
- n = Number of months

**Example Calculation**:
- Monthly SIP: ₹10,000
- Annual rate: 12% (0.12)
- Monthly rate: 0.01 (0.12/12)
- Time: 10 years (120 months)

**Calculation**: FV = 10000 × [((1.01)^120 - 1) / 0.01] × 1.01 = ₹23,00,396
