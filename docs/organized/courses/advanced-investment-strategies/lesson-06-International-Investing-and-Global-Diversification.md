## 1. Introduction to International Investing

### Benefits of International Investing

#### Portfolio Diversification

**Geographic Diversification**
- **Low Correlation**: Different economic cycles and market drivers
- **Crisis Hedge**: Domestic market stress may not affect international markets
- **Growth Opportunities**: Access to faster-growing economies
- **Currency Diversification**: Natural hedge against domestic currency risk

**Risk Reduction**
- **Correlation Benefits**: International markets often move independently
- **Economic Cycle Differences**: Staggered economic cycles across countries
- **Political Risk Diversification**: Reduced exposure to single political system
- **Market Size Benefits**: Access to world's largest and most liquid markets

#### Return Enhancement

**Growth Market Access**
- **Emerging Markets**: Higher growth potential in developing economies
- **Technology Leadership**: Access to global technology leaders
- **Resource Exposure**: Natural resources and commodity producers
- **Demographic Trends**: Aging populations in developed, young in emerging

**Sector Diversification**
- **Global Leaders**: Access to world's leading companies
- **Industry Specialization**: Industries concentrated in specific regions
- **Innovation Centers**: Silicon Valley, Chinese tech, European luxury
- **Brand Exposure**: Global consumer brands and franchises

#### Inflation Protection

**Currency Hedge**
- **Export Benefits**: Domestic currency weakness helps exporters
- **Asset Inflation**: Hard assets often denominated in foreign currencies
- **Global Pricing**: Inflation protection through global asset pricing
- **Real Returns**: Currency effects on real returns

### Challenges of International Investing

#### Currency Risk

**Exchange Rate Fluctuations**
- **Translation Risk**: Currency conversion impacts returns
- **Transaction Risk**: Currency movements during investment period
- **Economic Risk**: Currency values reflect economic fundamentals
- **Political Risk**: Government intervention in currency markets

**Currency Hedging Costs**
- **Forward Contracts**: Cost of currency hedging
- **Opportunity Cost**: Missed currency appreciation
- **Basis Risk**: Imperfect correlation between hedged and actual returns
- **Complexity**: Additional complexity in portfolio management

#### Regulatory and Tax Considerations

**Regulatory Differences**
- **Reporting Requirements**: Different disclosure and reporting standards
- **Investment Restrictions**: Limits on foreign investment in some markets
- **Liquidity Regulations**: Different market structure and liquidity
- **Compliance Costs**: Additional costs for regulatory compliance

**Tax Implications**
- **Double Taxation**: Tax treaties and foreign tax credits
- **Withholding Taxes**: Taxes on dividends and interest
- **Capital Gains**: Different tax treatment across countries
- **Tax Efficiency**: Structure investments for tax efficiency

#### Information and Research Challenges

**Information Asymmetry**
- **Language Barriers**: Research in foreign languages
- **Cultural Differences**: Understanding local business practices
- **Reporting Standards**: Different accounting and reporting standards
- **Time Zones**: Different trading hours and market cycles

**Research Costs**
- **Due Diligence**: Higher costs for international due diligence
- **Local Expertise**: Need for local market knowledge
- **Transaction Costs**: Higher brokerage and transaction costs

## 4. Currency Risk Management

### Currency Risk Fundamentals

#### Types of Currency Risk

**Transaction Risk**
- **Definition**: Risk from currency fluctuations during transaction period
- **Examples**: Import/export transactions, foreign investments
- **Time Frame**: Days to months
- **Management**: Forward contracts, natural hedging

**Translation Risk**
- **Definition**: Risk from currency conversion of foreign operations
- **Examples**: Consolidating foreign subsidiaries
- **Time Frame**: Quarterly/annual reporting
- **Management**: Currency hedging, natural hedging

**Economic Risk**
- **Definition**: Risk from long-term currency value changes
- **Examples**: Investment returns, competitive position
- **Time Frame**: Years
- **Management**: Strategic allocation, diversification

#### Currency Exposure Sources

**Direct Investments**
- **Foreign Stocks**: Direct equity investments
- **Foreign Bonds**: Fixed income investments
- **Foreign Real Estate**: Property investments
- **Foreign Businesses**: Direct business investments

**Indirect Investments**
- **International Funds**: Mutual funds and ETFs
- **Commodities**: Gold, oil denominated in USD
- **Global Companies**: Indian companies with global exposure
- **Export/Import**: Business operations exposure

### Currency Hedging Strategies

#### Forward Contracts

**Basic Forward Contract**
- **Structure**: Agreement to exchange currency at fixed rate
- **Maturity**: Customizable (weeks to years)
- **Credit Risk**: Counterparty risk
- **Liquidity**: Limited liquidity for long-dated contracts

**Forward Contract Example**
- **Investment**: $10,000 in US stocks
- **Current USD/INR**: 83.50
- **Forward Rate**: 83.80 (annual 4.8% premium)
- **Hedging Cost**: ₹3,000 for one year
- **Hedge Ratio**: 100% of exposure

#### Currency Options

**Currency Call Options**
- **Structure**: Right to buy foreign currency at strike rate
- **Cost**: Premium payment
- **Benefit**: Protection against adverse currency moves
- **Upside**: Benefit if currency moves favorably

**Currency Put Options**
- **Structure**: Right to sell foreign currency at strike rate
- **Cost**: Premium payment
- **Benefit**: Protection against currency depreciation
- **Upside**: Benefit if currency appreciates

#### Currency Swaps

**Currency Swap Structure**
- **Exchange**: Principal amounts in different currencies
- **Interest**: Exchange interest payments
- **Maturity**: Return principal at maturity
- **Duration**: Multi-year contracts

**Benefits**
- **Long-term Hedging**: Multi-year currency protection
- **Cost Effective**: Often lower cost than forwards
- **Flexibility**: Customizable terms
- **Credit Benefits**: May improve credit terms

### Hedging Decision Framework

#### When to Hedge

**Investment Horizon**
- **Short-term (<1 year)**: Usually hedge currency risk
- **Medium-term (1-5 years)**: Consider partial hedging
- **Long-term (>5 years)**: May not hedge, rely on diversification

**Market Conditions**
- **High Volatility**: Higher benefit from hedging
- **Trending Currency**: Consider directional hedging
- **Low Volatility**: May accept currency risk
- **Extreme Levels**: Consider contrarian hedging

**Investment Size**
- **Large Investments**: Higher benefit from hedging
- **Small Investments**: May accept currency risk
- **Portfolio Impact**: Consider portfolio-level exposure
- **Cost Considerations**: Hedging costs vs benefits

#### Hedge Ratio Determination

**Full Hedging (100%)**
- **Risk Averse**: Eliminate currency risk completely
- **Predictable Returns**: Focus on asset returns
- **Short-term**: Short investment horizons
- **High Volatility**: High currency volatility periods

**Partial Hedging (50-75%)**
- **Balanced Approach**: Balance risk and opportunity
- **Medium-term**: Medium investment horizons
- **Cost Consideration**: Moderate hedging costs
- **Market Conditions**: Normal market volatility

**No Hedging (0%)**
- **Opportunistic**: Accept currency risk for potential gains
- **Long-term**: Long investment horizons
- **Low Costs**: Minimize hedging costs
- **Diversification**: Rely on portfolio diversification

### Currency Risk Measurement

#### Value at Risk (VaR)

**Currency VaR Calculation**
- **Historical VaR**: Based on historical currency movements
- **Parametric VaR**: Normal distribution assumptions
- **Monte Carlo VaR**: Simulation-based approach
- **Stress Testing**: Extreme scenario analysis

**Example Currency VaR**
- **Investment**: ₹83.5 lakh in US stocks
- **Daily Volatility**: 0.8% (USD/INR)
- **95% VaR**: 1.65 × 0.8% × ₹83.5 lakh = ₹11.0 lakh
- **Interpretation**: 5% chance of daily loss > ₹11.0 lakh

#### Stress Testing

**Historical Scenarios**
- **2008 Crisis**: USD/INR moved from 40 to 50
- **2013 Taper**: USD/INR moved from 54 to 68
- **2018 Trade War**: USD/INR moved from 64 to 74
- **2022 Inflation**: USD/INR moved from 74 to 83

**Hypothetical Scenarios**
- **50% USD Appreciation**: USD/INR from 83 to 125
- **25% USD Depreciation**: USD/INR from 83 to 62
- **High Volatility**: 5% daily moves
- **Crisis Scenario**: 30% currency move in one month
